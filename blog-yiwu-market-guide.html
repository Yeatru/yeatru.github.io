Yiwu International Trade City: A Complete Buyer's Guide (2026)

May 25, 2026 Neil 7 min read

Why Yiwu Is the World's Sourcing Capital

Yiwu International Trade City (also called Futian Market) is the largest small commodity wholesale market in the world. With 75,000+ booths across 5 districts, it's the single most concentrated sourcing location for Amazon FBA, Shopify, and retail buyers. If you're looking for consumer goods at factory-direct prices, Yiwu should be your first stop.

Market Layout: 5 Districts Explained

The market is organized by product category, which makes sourcing efficient once you know where to go:

  • District 1: Toys, jewelry, hair accessories, crafts, Christmas decorations
  • District 2: Hardware, kitchen tools, stationery, office supplies, sporting goods
  • District 3: Makeup tools, eyewear, buttons, zippers, sewing supplies
  • District 4: Socks, underwear, scarves, hats, gloves, belts
  • District 5: Automotive accessories, pet supplies, home textiles, luggage

Best Time to Visit Yiwu

The market is open year-round, but timing matters:

  • March-April and September-October: Best weather, new product launches
  • Avoid: Chinese New Year (1-2 weeks closure), National Day holiday (crowded)
  • Daily hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM, most booths close on Sunday afternoon

Getting There: Transportation Tips

Yiwu is accessible via:

  • Yiwu Airport (YIW): Domestic flights from major Chinese cities
  • High-speed train: 1.5 hrs from Hangzhou, 2.5 hrs from Shanghai
  • Market shuttle: Free shuttle connects districts inside the market

Pro Tip: Stay in the Futian District near the market. Most hotels offer free breakfast and are within walking distance of the main entrance. Book 2-3 days minimum — you'll need more time than you think.

Negotiation Strategy for First-Time Buyers

Yiwu suppliers are used to international buyers, but negotiation skills still matter:

  1. Ask for the "FOB Ningbo" price, not the retail price
  2. Mention your expected order volume (even if it's small)
  3. Get quotes from at least 3-5 booths selling the same product
  4. Don't accept the first price — 10-20% reduction is normal with volume
  5. Ask for samples before placing bulk orders

MOQs: What to Expect

MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity) varies by product and supplier:

  • Small accessories: 100-500 pcs per style
  • Household items: 50-200 pcs per style
  • Textiles: 500-1,000 pcs per design
  • Mixed orders: Many suppliers accept mixed products from the same category

Working with a Local Yiwu Agent: Is It Worth It?

For first-time buyers, a local agent usually saves more than their fee. Here's why:

  • They know which booths are factories vs. trading companies
  • They negotiate in Mandarin and get better prices
  • They handle quality inspection and shipping logistics
  • They can consolidate orders from multiple suppliers
  • They provide product photos and samples without you traveling

Payment Methods: Safe Options

Stick to these payment methods for security:

  • 30% deposit + 70% before shipment (standard for established suppliers)
  • Trade Assurance through Alibaba for first orders
  • Bank transfer (T/T) for larger orders
  • Avoid: 100% upfront payment to new suppliers

Final Checklist Before You Leave

  1. Collected business cards from all suppliers you're interested in
  2. Taken photos of products with booth numbers
  3. Confirmed MOQ, price, lead time, and payment terms in writing
  4. Arranged sample shipping (DHL/FedEx typically 3-5 days)
  5. Verified supplier has export license and can handle FOB terms
